Chlarity




One of my first classes at the University of Michigan was History 195, a writing class that examined global humanitarianism in the modern era. Among the required readings was Anand Giridharadas’ Winners Take All: The Elite Charade of Changing the Worldi, which explained that there exists a tendency in some humanitarian organizations/charities to keep as many resources in their own hands as possible, rather than using them to create meaningful change for the people whom they had promised to serve. In short, these entities would intentionally do ineffectual or self-serving work to perpetuate the plight of the needy so that they could continue to profit off of them.

Discerning between organizations that follow through on their promises and those that don’t is a difficult task, so when a couple of friends and I were assigned a project where we were supposed to solve an issue, this seemed like a natural fit. Our solution was to create an app (Chlarity) that would allow organizations that DO honor their promises to more intricately display where their funding goes. That way, they could build consumer trust and promote their brand. Organizations would sign on as partners, and then get direct access to their target audience as well as other organizations for collaboration.

The purpose of having this on my website is that I believe that it was a good exercise in prototyping, identifying user pain points, and collaborating to decide what product features desirable and technically feasible.
